"Future's Made of Virtual Insanity" - Jamiroquai


With the lockdown over the summer, I started looking into what people get up to outside of games in VR. As it turns out, the headsets have so much versatility when you can apply them to product design, civil engineering and so much more.

Being able to visualize, construct and prototype ideas quickly and as cost-efficient as possible are ideas we're taught as third year software engineers. While these are concepts I'm sure carry across sectors like product design, VR allows creative teams to showcase their ideas in a tangible and visual manner without needing to spend money on physical resources to potential customers.

Take one of the biggest racecar companies in the world; McLaren. Using VR, they're able to craft models and design their prototype work without needing large physical spaces to work with moulds or produce swathes of conceptual art when simply chasing an idea rather than the finished product.
I mean heck, take a look at my pure enjoyment when experiencing VR for the first time!
