Assistive Technology


Before I pursued by degree in software, I was raising money each year for a charity called Extra Life. They fundraise to help support children's hospitals across the US, one of which was for kids with physical disorders. Because I raised money by streaming video games on the internet, I started to think about how does someone with cerebral palsy get to play video games?

As a sufferer of dyspraxia, I recognized that my limitations didn't extend towards video games; it was why I had taken up the hobby in the first place. It was all hand-eye coordination but I didn't need balance like for skateboarding, I didn't need upper body strength for the few sports I did enjoy. So how does someone who has none of these abilities enjoy even the most menial of games?

As it turns out, there's already a wealth of work and dedication from organisations like Enable Ireland and ATMakers. In fact, Microsoft even have their own brand new Xbox Adaptive Controller to provide an interface for makers to map custom components onto the control schemes for their console. Check it out below.
